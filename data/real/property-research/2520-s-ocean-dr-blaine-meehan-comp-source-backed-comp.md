## Market trends and quick-flip conditions

- **Scarcity:** Small Hutchinson Island/South Beach lots are thinly traded. County recorded sales show clean island lots clearing in the $22–$40/sf band in 2024–2025, materially above ordinary Fort Pierce mainland dirt.
- **Immediate-neighborhood split:** Surfside Harbor has many cheap/no-address/no-building/raw parcel records and low recorded trades. That is not automatically fatal, but it means buyers will ask whether the subject has legal buildability, real water/sewer/tap access, driveway access, and practical C-3 use.
- **Buyer pool:** Best buyer lane is not a generic subdivision builder. First calls should go to same-city coastal/custom builders, boutique commercial operators, restaurant/hospitality users, small developers, and local agents with South Beach/Hutchinson dirt buyers.
- **C-3 commercial is double-edged:** Scarcity premium if a buyer can use it; negative if parking/setbacks/flood/coastal codes make the site too small for practical commercial use.
- **Liquidity:** For wholesaling, expect a narrower buyer pool than standard residential infill. You need buyer validation before exceeding ~$205k seller basis.
- **2026 risk posture:** Higher carry/insurance/construction costs and flood/coastal code scrutiny make buyers selective. This strengthens the case for a discounted quick-close offer even when retail comps look strong.
