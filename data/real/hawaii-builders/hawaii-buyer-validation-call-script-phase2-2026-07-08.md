# Hawaii Buyer Validation Call Script + Buy-Box Capture

## Call opener
Good afternoon, my name is Okeito. I saw you are active building in Hawaii. I help source off-market land opportunities and I’m trying to make sure anything I send is actually useful for builders. Are you currently buying or reviewing buildable lots?

## Fast qualification
1. Which islands, towns, ZIPs, or subdivisions are you actively buying in right now?
2. What lot sizes work for you? Minimum and maximum?
3. What is the max lot price you can pay when the lot fits? Please separate raw-land price from site-work/utility budget.
4. Do you buy scattered infill lots, finished/platted lots, acreage, or only lots inside active communities?
5. How fast can you close if title is clean and the lot fits?
6. What instantly kills a Hawaii land deal for you: lava zone, flood, wetlands, slope, road access, water meter, septic, power distance, zoning, CPR/subdivision, title, HOA/CC&Rs, shoreline/SMA?
7. Do you need county permit history, DCCA license/contractor proof, survey, staking, topo, wastewater, or utility letters before reviewing?
8. Who should receive land packages? Name, role, email, phone.
9. If I brought a lot at your target price, what spread/assignment room would keep it worthwhile for both sides?

## Hawaii-specific deal-killer prompts
- Big Island: lava zone, catchment/water, cesspool/septic feasibility, unpaved/private roads, HELCO distance, subdivision road fees.
- Oahu: zoning/CPR, sewer/water meter, DPP permit feasibility, teardown vs vacant-lot preference, infill parking/setbacks.
- Maui: water meter priority list, wildfire/rebuild constraints, SMA/shoreline, septic/wastewater, upcountry slope/access.
- Kauai: septic/wastewater, flood/SMA, access/bridges, vegetation/slope, county permit feasibility.

## Close
If I send you a blind lot profile first — area, size, zoning, access/utilities, risk flags, and price band without seller identity — would you tell me quickly whether it fits?
