# Contracts + Closing Research for LandFlip OS

Date: 2026-06-17
Owner: IriSys / Okeito
Purpose: prepare a practical contract + title-company operating stack for buyer-first vacant-land wholesaling / assignment operations.

> Not legal advice. This is an operating research memo. Before sending contracts to sellers or buyers, have a real estate attorney licensed in the target state review the final packet.

## Executive answer

A single "universal" real estate contract that safely works in every U.S. state is the wrong target. The durable target is a **state-adapted contract packet** built from a common operating spine:

1. assignable vacant-land purchase agreement,
2. clear buyer-as-principal language,
3. inspection / due-diligence contingency,
4. title / marketable-title contingency,
5. escrow/title-company closing instructions,
6. assignment-of-contract form,
7. wholesaling / assignment disclosure where required or prudent,
8. state/county addenda for local rules.

For Lehigh Acres / Lee County, use a **Florida-specific vacant land purchase contract** and a **Florida-authorized title company or closing attorney**. A national title company is fine only if it can lawfully close Florida transactions and issue/coordinate Florida title insurance.

## Research findings

### 1. Wholesaling is state-regulated; the danger is acting like a broker without a license

The common compliant theory is: you are not selling someone else's property; you are buying a property under contract as a principal and then assigning or reselling your contract/equitable interest.

High-risk behavior:

- advertising the property itself before you own it,
- collecting a fee for connecting buyer and seller,
- representing another party,
- hiding that you intend to assign,
- using non-assignable forms,
- failing to follow state/city disclosure or licensing laws.

Evidence/examples:

- Oklahoma Real Estate Commission publishes an official wholesaling resource PDF. That alone signals states treat wholesaling as a regulated compliance area, not a casual paperwork trick.
  - Source: https://oklahoma.gov/content/dam/ok/en/orec/documents/resources/Wholesaling%20Resource1.pdf
- Philadelphia requires a Residential Property Wholesaler License. The official city page lists the license, who can apply, and requirements.
  - Source: https://www.phila.gov/services/permits-violations-licenses/get-a-license/business-licenses/rental-and-property/get-a-residential-property-wholesaler-license/
- State-by-state wholesaling guides consistently flag licensing/disclosure/assignment-law variation. These are not primary law, but useful monitoring sources.
  - Source example: https://landwholesalinglaws.com/
  - Source example: https://dealrun.ai/compliance

Operating implication: LandFlip OS should never assume "assignment is legal everywhere." It should track target state and show a compliance status before seller outreach.

### 2. The contract packet should be modular, not universal

The reusable spine can be stable, but the actual signed form should be state-specific.

Core clauses we need in the seller purchase agreement:

- parties and legal names,
- property legal description / parcel ID / site address,
- purchase price,
- earnest money and escrow holder,
- closing date,
- title company / closing agent selection,
- marketable title requirement,
- title objection cure period,
- inspection / feasibility / due diligence period,
- buyer cancellation right during due diligence,
- prorations / taxes / assessments,
- seller authority and ownership representations,
- no undisclosed liens / encumbrances representation,
- access / ingress / egress representation where possible,
- assignability clause,
- closing costs allocation,
- default/remedies,
- electronic signatures allowed,
- state-specific disclosures/addenda.

For vacant land specifically, add diligence checks:

- zoning / future land use,
- road access,
- utilities availability,
- wetlands/environmental constraints,
- flood zone,
- HOA/deed restrictions,
- survey/legal access issues,
- buildability and permitting feasibility.

### 3. Assignment must be explicit

Minimum language concept for attorney review:

> Buyer may assign this Agreement and its rights under this Agreement to an entity, partner, affiliate, end buyer, or third-party assignee without Seller's further consent. Buyer remains responsible for Buyer's obligations unless released in writing.

But this is only a draft concept. Some states/forms/contracts restrict assignment, and some buyers/title companies prefer double close or novation instead of assignment.

Operating implication: each seller record should carry:

- contract assignable: yes/no/unknown,
- assignment disclosed: yes/no/not required but recommended,
- assignment method: assignment / double close / novation,
- attorney-reviewed state packet: yes/no.

### 4. Disclosure is becoming the safer default

Even where not required, disclosure lowers litigation and licensing risk.

Minimum practical disclosure concept:

> Buyer is purchasing for investment purposes and may close directly, assign Buyer's contractual rights, or resell the property. Seller acknowledges Buyer is not acting as Seller's real estate broker or agent.

This protects the principal-buyer posture: we are not representing the seller. We are making an offer.

### 5. Title/escrow must be authorized in the property state

A title company does not have to be physically located in the exact same city/county as the land, but it needs the authority and capability to close in the **state where the property is located**.

Reasons:

- title insurance is state-regulated,
- escrow/trust handling is state-regulated,
- recording rules and transfer taxes are local/state-specific,
- some states require attorney involvement for closing or deed preparation,
- vacant land has local title defects: access, legal description, tax liens, municipal liens, HOA restrictions, code liens.

Florida evidence:

- Florida Statute 626.8473 says a title insurance agent may act as escrow agent for funds disbursed in connection with real estate closing transactions involving title insurance, provided the licensed/appointed title insurance agent complies with statutory requirements.
  - Source: https://www.flsenate.gov/Laws/Statutes/2022/626.8473
- Florida has a dedicated Title Insurance Agents part in Chapter 626.
  - Source: https://www.flsenate.gov/Laws/Statutes/2025/Chapter626/Part_V

ALTA evidence:

- ALTA is the national trade association for title insurance agents, abstracters, and underwriters, and maintains industry infrastructure such as the ALTA Registry.
  - Source: https://www.alta.org/registry/resources-getting-started

Operating implication: for Lehigh Acres, use a Florida title company / Florida closing attorney / national title provider with Florida authority. Do not use an Australia-local conveyancer or random out-of-state title office unless they can close Florida property through a Florida-licensed operation.

## Recommended contract stack

### Seller-side packet

1. State-specific vacant land purchase agreement.
2. Assignment/disclosure addendum.
3. Inspection/due-diligence addendum if not built into the base form.
4. Title/escrow instruction sheet.
5. Seller intake checklist.
6. Wire-fraud warning / secure escrow instructions.

### Buyer/end-buyer packet

1. Buyer buy-box validation form.
2. Proof-of-funds / closing-capacity request.
3. Assignment agreement or double-close instruction, depending on transaction.
4. Buyer acknowledgement of due diligence responsibility.
5. Closing timeline and earnest-money instruction.

### Title-company packet

1. Executed seller purchase agreement.
2. Assignment agreement or double-close instruction.
3. Buyer/end-buyer identity and vesting details.
4. Escrow deposit proof.
5. Parcel ID, legal description, owner details.
6. Request for title search, municipal lien search if applicable, tax proration, closing statement, and title commitment.

## State-readiness checklist before calls

Before seller outreach in any market, mark:

- Target state selected.
- Attorney-reviewed contract packet exists for that state.
- Assignability confirmed.
- Required state/city wholesaling disclosures identified.
- License/registration risk reviewed.
- Title/closing company authorized in that state selected.
- Escrow holder named in contract.
- Assignment vs double-close path chosen.
- Seller script avoids broker language.
- Buyer script validates closing capacity before seller contract.

## Florida / Lee County recommendation

For our selected Lehigh Acres market:

- Use a Florida vacant land contract as the base, not a generic national template.
- Have a Florida real estate attorney review our assignment/disclosure addendum.
- Select 2–3 Florida land-investor-friendly title companies before seller calls.
- Ask each title company:
  - Do you handle vacant land assignments?
  - Do you allow assignment fees on the settlement statement?
  - Do you support double close if assignment is unsuitable?
  - Can you close Lee County vacant land remotely?
  - What earnest-money minimum do you require?
  - What municipal/tax/lien searches do you run for Lee County?
  - What exact contract language do you prefer for assignments?

For Lehigh, the fastest safe path is:
